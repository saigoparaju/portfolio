Part-1 Basic understanding of how a digital camera works

The light falls on the flip up mirror and is reflected towards the viewing system which helps the user to view the image through the view finder. Once the shutter is clicked the flip up mirror goes down allowing the light to fall on the sensor. The sensor captures the light and develops the image digitally. More on how the sensor creates the image is known in future classes.

Part-2 A digital cameras automatic and manual settings

When a digital camera is set to automatic mode it automatically sets the aperture and shutter speed for you according to the surrounding light. Whereas when set in manual you will have the flexibility to choose your own aperture and shutter speed. Most of the pro photographers choose manual settings as it gives more control over the image. You must be wondering what actually is an aperture or a shutter speed.Here you go..
  • Aperture: The size of the opening in the camera.
  • Shutter speed: The amount of time that light can pass through the aperture.
