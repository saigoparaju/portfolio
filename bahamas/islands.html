  • Abacos

    The Abaco islands of Bahamas with its own islands surrounded by calm blue waters are known as the world's top most sailing and boating destinations. It has a 120 mile long chain of islands with Great Abaco and Little Abaco islands. With well developed hotels, restaurants, bars Abaco is a perfect holiday destination in Bahamas.

    Acklins is a small island with 92 miles length and 4 miles wide utmost. Though, lessly populated with only 400 residents there are many things to do in this islands. Snorkeling, scuba diving and fishing are the main attractions of this islands. The shallow waters of Acklins are famouse for bonefish expeditions.

  • Berry Islands

    The Berry Islands are referred as "The fish bowl of Bahamas". It has a wide range of fish types some of which are billfish, tuna, groupers, tigerfish, wahoo etc. It is the best place for underwater adventures and an endless source of in-water experience. It has many abandoned and privately owned cays, old churches and authentic food for the visitiors to explore.

  • Bimini

    Bimini islands are famous for the underwater cliffs that are thousands of feet below the blue water. The gulf stream that washes the coral reefs of Bimini are the living habitat for Dolphins to sea turtles. It has a lake called "Fountain of Youth" which is known for making people healthier with its sulfurs and lithium.

    San Salvador is known as the origin of Bahamas. It is named as Guanahani by the native locals. It is a 200 mile island which is at a distance of an hour fly from Florida.
